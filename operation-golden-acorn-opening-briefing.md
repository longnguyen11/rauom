# Operation: Golden Acorn - Opening Briefing

Estimated read time: 8-10 minutes

Before we start, I need everyone to do one quick thing.

Look at the teams sitting around you.

Take a good look.

Because at least two of them are going to help you solve a secret this week...

...and at least one of them will stab you in the back. Honestly, I hope **all of them will stab you in the back.**

Welcome, agents...

to **Operation: Golden Acorn.**

---

## The Objective

The goal of this event is simple:

**Earn the most stars by the end of the week.**

Stars determine the winning team.

Now before we get into the rules, I want to set expectations.

All the challenges are completely made up.

The situations are ridiculous.

And the points don't matter.

**Except the stars. The stars absolutely matter.**

---

## How Each Day Works

Each day follows roughly the same structure.

On most days, we start with a **morning team challenge**.

Today is a Day 1 setup flow:

- opening briefing
- 10-minute secret + decoy submission window
- then the first morning challenge

These will be things like building towers out of office supplies, solving puzzles, or doing something collaborative as a team.

These challenges award stars and sometimes Bitacorn.

And just so everyone knows - the judging process will be extremely scientific and completely unbiased.

Which mostly means I'll probably go with whatever seems funniest.

---

## Daily Missions

After the morning challenge, each team will receive a set of **daily missions**.

Two of those missions will involve **discovering secrets from another team**.

Every team in this room will submit a few secrets right after this briefing.

These are things like:

- favorite lunch order
- favorite coffee
- most-used emoji
- favorite weekend activity

Your mission will sometimes be to **figure out another team's secret**.

So sometime today someone may casually walk up to you and ask something like:

"Hey... what do you normally order for lunch?"

Just remember...

**you may be talking to a spy.**

---

## How Secrets Work

There are three main ways to discover a secret.

First, you can **ask around and deduce it socially**.

Talk to people.  
Observe habits.  
Piece together clues.

Sometimes the simplest method works.

---

Second, you can **trade for the secret**.

Teams can negotiate with each other using Bitacorn.

If someone is willing to sell a secret, you can buy it.

So bribery is technically allowed this week.

Just remember: **Bitacorn only. HR prefers we keep it that way.**

---

Third, if negotiations fail, you can trigger what we call a **fallback guess**.

This can only be triggered **captain to captain** between teams.

When fallback is triggered, the defending team will provide **three possible answers**.

Only one of those answers is correct.

Your team gets **one guess**, and that guess becomes your mission answer.

---

Now here's where things get interesting.

Different teams **will receive different sets of options**.

So if two teams compare notes, you might be able to **deduce the real answer together**.

Which means alliances are possible.

But remember...

only the **first team to submit the correct answer** gets the bonus.

So cooperation is allowed...

**but betrayal is also part of the spy genre.**

---

## Secret Rewards

When your team correctly discovers a secret, you earn **2 stars**.

And when multiple teams are chasing the same secret, the **first team to submit the correct answer** earns an additional **+20 Bitacorn bonus**.

Other teams can still complete the mission and earn the stars.

But only the first team gets the Bitacorn reward.

So speed matters.

If you see another team suddenly running across the office...

they might be racing you to submit an answer.

Or they might just really need coffee.

---

## Bitacorn

Every team begins the game with **200 Bitacorn**.

You can earn more Bitacorn from:

- impromptu challenges
- secret discoveries
- and other rewards throughout the week.

You can spend Bitacorn to:

- buy secrets
- negotiate deals
- and most importantly...

the **final star auction**.

---

## The Final Star Auction

At the end of the event we will hold a **star auction**.

Teams can use their Bitacorn to bid for additional stars.

But there's one twist.

You will **not know how many auction rounds there will be**.

So you'll have to decide:

Do you spend early...

or save everything for the end?

Or panic and spend everything in round one.

That strategy is also completely valid.

---

## The Spirit of the Game

This event runs on a **light honor code**.

Be competitive.

Make alliances.

Trade secrets.

Mislead each other.

But most importantly - have fun with it.

The best part of this event will probably be the stories that come out of it.

And I fully expect at least one team to accidentally reveal their own secret within the first hour.

---

## Immediate Next Step (Day 1)

Before we start the first challenge, each team will take about **10 minutes** to finalize and submit your secrets.

For each secret, submit:

- the **true answer**
- a couple of **plausible decoy answers** for fallback prompts

Make sure your teammates know the **official answer** for each secret.

While you do this, confirm your **Team Captain** (captain-only can trigger fallback), and optionally name a **Vice Captain**.

Once secrets are submitted, they are **locked for the rest of the event**.

---

## Final Send-Off

After the 10-minute submission window closes, your missions will appear and the first challenge begins.

Start talking to people.

Start making deals.

Start uncovering secrets.

And remember...

**the office is now an intelligence battlefield.**

Good luck, agents.

**Operation: Golden Acorn begins now.**
